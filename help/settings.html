The GarneX clock has four (4) settings, for ease of comunication, they are called: Roman, Quadrant Only, Rotate, and Smooth. The smooth setting is toggled by left clicking anywhere on the face of the clock. You may step through all combinations of the other three by holding the control key down while left clicking on the face of the clock.

The default clock face:
Roman: Off, Quadrant Only: On, Rotate: Off.

 
The Roman setting determines which type of numbers will be displayed on the clock: Roman or the more common Arabic.

 
The Quadrant Only setting determines which numbers will get displayed. Whether all 12 numbers will be displayed or just the four numbers on the quadrants.

 
The Rotate settings determines how the nubers will be drawn around the face: will they be rotated, which is the common practice clock faces with Roman Numerals or straight up and down, which is more common for clock faces with arabic numbers.

The different face:
Roman: On, Quadrant Only: Off, Rotate: On.

 
The Smooth setting determine how the second hand will move: wether it approximates a sweep or smooth movement, or steps with each second, as most quartz movements do. In truth, since all computers are digital machines, the hand will step. Setting the smooth on will cause small steps, to approximate a smooth movement at the cost of additional processing. If you are concerned with performance while the clock is running, turn smooth off.
